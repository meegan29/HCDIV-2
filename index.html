Assignment 2: Critique of Visualizations

Visualization 1

This purpose of this chart was to represent the sentiments of White and Black people regading the O.J. Simpson murder trial verdict.

Critique Improvements
  • The y-axis begins at 10%, which slightly skews the visual representation of the data's area.
  • The y-axis should begin at zero.
  • The labels on each data point does not accurately line up with the percentage points on the y-axis, misleading its viewers into believing that there is a greater proportion for each data point than depicted.
  • The labels should be correctly depicted
  • The data points should be accurately reflected on the scale of y-axis.
  • The colours and colour gradation of the graph does not add any additional information, which may result in unnecessary noise and cognitive load for the viewer.
  • Data points should be solid-coloured and correspond to the data labels (e.g., Black people -> Black, White people -> White)
  • In the context of this situation, absolute values are not critical to understanding the implications of the data.
  • Viewers would have to process the absolute height of multiple bars before they are able to make comparisons.
  • The current clustered bar chart may also mislead viewers by implying a trend across responses.
  • In order to focus on the proportional relationship between the two groups and their relative responses, a 100% stacked bar chart would be more effective as multiple bars are consolidated into one per group
  • With a 100% stacked bar chart, it will be easier to visually compare proportions across groups as the total length will be equal
  • Because each bar represents 100%, the viewer's attention will be directed to the distribution of categories rather than the absolute differences.

Visualization 2

This stacked area graph comes from the NYT article comparing power produced by energy source between 2001 and 2023.

Critique Improvements
  • Overlapping and crowding of smaller categories such as petroleum and solar, making it difficult to identify and read.
  • Larger and clearer labels so that users so that searching for labels is less effortful.
  • The y-axis is not labelled, making it not immediately clear that it is a 100% stacked area chart
  • Adding a y-axis with percentages on the left side of the graph to show proportions.
  • Lack of granularity for large sections like for "Coal" and "Natural Gas". It's difficult to identify where the changes span over the years.
  • Adding faint gridlines or additional reference markers could help viewers better gauge changes.
  • Percentage markers only appear at the start and end of the chart.
  • Percentage markers are overlapping for "Coal" and "Nuclear" sources, increasing the effort required to differentiate between which label represents which data point.
  • Percentage markers should appear more periodically to accurately depict the proportion and change in proportion between the categories.
  • Percentage markers should not overlap and should only be contained within the data they represent.
  • The chart's purpose appears to be emphasizing the change in ranking of energy sources over time, but this may result in a visual overload as there are overlaps in data, consequently making it difficult to compare trends over time.
  • The 100% stacked area graph should not have any overlaps, and should not have any gaps in between data areas.
