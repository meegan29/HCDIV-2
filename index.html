Assignment 2: Critique of Visualizations

Visualization 1

This chart comes from CBC's (Canadian Broadcasting Corporation) 2018-2019 Annual Report. The main purpose of this report is to disclose sources of funding received by the CBC.

Critique Improvements
  • X-axis represents the time-series data the opposite direction, making it unintuitive to read as it goes against the natural flow of time
  • X-axis should be ordered from 2017-2018 followed by 2018-2019
  • The y-axis "jumps" from $700M to $1,700M. This makes the scale visually misleading, especially depicted by the $1,213.7M government funding area from 2018-19, which appears significantly smaller than revenue generated by CBC depsite an almost 700M difference.
  • The y-axis should be scaled appropriately to show an accurate depiction the total revenue generated.
  • Given the height of the column, it may be more appropriate to display it horizontally using a bar chart.
  • The data has been split into three different columns to break down the funding generated for "Total", "Revenue", and "Advertising Revenue".
  • This is misleading because there appears to be more sources of funding.
  • For example, labelling and displaying "Advertising", "Digital" and "Television" in different colors misrepresents the data as seperate levels of categorical data, when in fact "Digital" and "Television" are a subcategory of "Advertising".
  • Instead of using multiple stacked bar charts, the data may be more accurately represented and compared using a standard bar chart, representing each categorical data as a column on its own.
  • Categories should also be split into subcategories. For example, "Advertising" should not be represented in the graph, and "Digital" and "Television" could instead be accurately relabelled as "Digital Advertising" and "Television Advertising".

Visualization 2

This stacked area graph comes from the New York Times (NYT) article comparing power produced by energy source between 2001 and 2023.

Critique Improvements
  • Overlapping and crowding of smaller categories such as petroleum and solar, making it difficult to identify and read.
  • Larger and clearer labels so that users so that searching for labels is less effortful.
  • The y-axis is not labelled, making it not immediately clear that it is a 100% stacked area chart
  • Adding a y-axis with percentages on the left side of the graph to show proportions.
  • Lack of granularity for large sections like for "Coal" and "Natural Gas". It's difficult to identify where the changes span over the years.
  • Adding faint gridlines or additional reference markers could help viewers better gauge changes.
  • Percentage markers only appear at the start and end of the chart.
  • Percentage markers are overlapping for "Coal" and "Nuclear" sources, increasing the effort required to differentiate between which label represents which data point.
  • Percentage markers should appear more periodically to accurately depict the proportion and change in proportion between the categories.
  • Percentage markers should not overlap and should only be contained within the data they represent.
  • The chart's purpose appears to be emphasizing the change in ranking of energy sources over time, but this may result in a visual overload as there are overlaps in data, consequently making it difficult to compare trends over time.
  • The 100% stacked area graph should not have any overlaps, and should not have any gaps in between data areas.
